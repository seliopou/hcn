BREAKING: Men Walk on Moon

20 July 1969—Neil Armstrong became the first man to set foot on a celestial object, closely followed by Buzz Aldrin. The third crew-member manned the command module. His name has been lost to history.

This was the first step on man’s journey to colonize the cosmos. It also marked the beginning of the US space program’s steady decline and waning public interest in space exploration. However, today’s events set the foundation for commercial space flight.

The future of space travel remains speculative, so we will not speculate. Once it happens, our commentary will follow within a half-century.
